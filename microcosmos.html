Microcosmos

The Foldscope is an extremely low cost paper microscope that uses a glass bead to get up to 500x zoom, or up to 5000x zoom if a smartphone lens is attached. Unfortunately, sharing smartphone images from a Foldscope required uploading and posting them to the Microcosmos Wordpress site, which meant users (1) often wouldn't post their images (2) had trouble formatting their posts, and (3) the scale of images was rarely conveyed. The Microcosmos App integrates with the website by calibratating and accounting for the zoom on a user's phone, posting users' images in an informative and aesthetic layout, and showing users content from the site in a clean and modern environent.

Project Background

This project represented my first foree into freelance mobile app design, so I did my best to fully lay out the interface before putting anything into code! I used Sketch to iteratively mock up what the screens would look like, before moving into React-Native to bring it to life. While the final details of the application are still in-development (Foldscope has decided to migrate their database, which may not be completed until May), most of the front end development has been completed.
